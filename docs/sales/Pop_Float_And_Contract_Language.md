# Pop Float and Contract Language

## Purpose
This note captures the business and contract position for Pop-funded CPG rebate campaigns.

## Core Position
Campaign funding must be structured as **prepaid campaign credits**, not escrow in the strict regulated sense.

This matters because Pop intends to:
- Accept upfront campaign funding from vendors
- Sweep or hold those funds in Pop operating or treasury accounts
- Retain any resulting **float / interest / yield**
- Remain obligated to honor valid shopper rebates
- Offer vendors unused-credit **rollover or refund** rights per contract terms

## Required Initial Contract Language
The initial vendor agreement must explicitly state:
- Campaign funding is a **prepaid credit purchase** for Pop campaign services
- Funds may be held, swept, or commingled in Pop-controlled operating or treasury accounts
- Pop retains any associated **float, interest, or treasury yield**
- Vendor rights are limited to:
  - campaign delivery
  - reporting / reconciliation
  - unused-credit rollover
  - unused-credit refund, if applicable under the agreement
- Funds are **not** held in a vendor-owned escrow account unless Pop later chooses a regulated escrow structure

## Operational Implications
- Finance should track campaign credits separately from shopper payout obligations
- Product should support campaign `start_date`, `end_date`, rollover, and refund workflows
- Legal should review this structure for payments, stored value, money transmission, and consumer-payout implications before launch

## Planning Note
Veryfi and payout-processing costs should be modeled as direct campaign servicing costs when pricing rebate programs.
